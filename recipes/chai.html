Chai

Description

Chai, a cherished beverage deeply rooted in Pakistani and Indian cultures, transcends its role as a mere drink to become a ritual, a social connector, and a symbol of hospitality. This aromatic concoction is crafted from a blend of robust black tea, infused with spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves, and sweetened with milk and sugar, resulting in a distinctive and comforting flavor profile. Chai's significance extends beyond its taste; it holds a central place in daily life, fostering conversations and providing a warm respite during various occasions. Whether enjoyed at bustling street stalls or in the serene atmosphere of homes, chai serves as more than a beverage; it encapsulates a cultural tradition, offering a comforting embrace that unites communities across borders.

Ingredients

  • 3 tsp of black tea leaves
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1-3 tbsp of sweetener of choice (optional)
  • 3-4 crushed cardamom pods
  • 1 one-inch cinnamon stick

Steps

  1. Fill a pot with 1 cup of water, 3 tsp of black tea leaves, 3-4 crushed cardamom pods, and 1 one-inch cinnamon stick.
  2. Wait until the water in the pot boils, then add 1 cup of milk.
  3. Once the tea with milk boils and starts to aggressivly bubble up, turn down the heat to a simmer.
  4. After 3 minutes, turn the heat off and add the sweetener of choice (optional)
  5. Finally, pour the chai into cups and enjoy.
